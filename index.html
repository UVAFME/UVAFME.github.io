UVAFME is an individual-based gap model that simulates the establishment, growth, and mortality of individual trees on independent plots of a forested landscape. Originally developed as FAREAST for use in boreal Eurasia (see Yan & Shugart 2005), the model has since been updated for use in boreal Russia, the southern Rocky Mountains, the eastern US, as well as boreal North America.
